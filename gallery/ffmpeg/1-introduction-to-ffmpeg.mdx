## What is FFmpeg?

FFmpeg is a powerful, open-source command-line tool designed for processing audio and video files. Created by Fabrice Bellard in 2000, its name stands for "Fast Forward" followed by the "Moving Picture Experts Group" file format. [video:00:00]

It serves as a foundational component in a vast array of modern applications and platforms, enabling robust multimedia handling capabilities.

## Core Capabilities and Features

FFmpeg boasts an extensive feature set, capable of decoding, encoding, transcoding, multiplexing, demultiplexing, streaming, filtering, and playing virtually any multimedia file format. It supports over 100 different codecs, making it incredibly versatile for various multimedia tasks. [video:00:15]

Key functionalities include:
*   **Decode/Encode:** Convert raw data to/from compressed formats.
*   **Transcode:** Convert from one encoded format to another.
*   **Multiplex/Demultiplex:** Combine/separate elementary streams (audio, video, subtitles) into/from a container format.
*   **Filter:** Apply a wide range of audio and video effects and transformations.
*   **Stream/Play:** Handle live streams and playback media directly.

## Widespread Adoption

Due to its versatility and robustness, FFmpeg is widely adopted across the industry. It powers multimedia functionality in popular web browsers like Google Chrome, professional 3D animation software like Blender, and major video platforms such as YouTube and Vimeo. [video:00:10]
